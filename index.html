Good to know

A few quick answers

Why is the application so long?

Because this is a job you do almost entirely on your own, and we can only take a few people out on a trial day. The questionnaire helps us understand who you really are — how you work alone, your eye for detail, and whether the honest version of the role suits you. Ten to fifteen minutes now saves everyone time later.

Do I need experience with flowers?

Not at all. If you're warm, reliable, organised and happy to work on your own, we'll teach you everything about the arrangements and the route. The one thing we can't teach on the job is being comfortable with a smartphone and Google Maps — you'll use them every day to find clients and plan your run — so you'll want to be confident there. Most of our best reps had never arranged a flower before.

How many hours is it, really?

It starts at about 6 days a month — genuinely part-time and flexible during daytime hours. You're welcome to keep it that way. And if you'd like more, there's a clear path to grow it to three days a week, or even full-time, as you bring on new clients and expand the territory.

Is it physically demanding?

There's real lifting — some vases can be heavy (think 6–10 kg) and you'll be carrying them in and out of the van through the day, on your feet and moving. It suits someone fit and active. In summer it's a joy; in winter you'll be out in the Vancouver rain too, so it helps to genuinely not mind the weather.

What does "growing it" actually involve?

Offering local businesses a free flower trial, then dropping back a week later to see if they'd like to keep it. It's honestly the easiest thing to sell — you're giving people free flowers, and the flowers do the convincing. But you do need to be happy knocking on doors. If that's you, the territory (and your hours) can grow a lot.

What happens after I finish the questionnaire?

We read every single one personally. If you look like a strong fit, we'll call you for a friendly chat and then arrange a real day out on the road with Brandon, our current rep, so you can see exactly what the role's like before anyone commits.
